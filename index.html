Welcome to UMASS Boston

This website was built for incoming students who would like some valuable tips on how to succeed in college. High-school and College are two very different things. College requires more self-control especially when it comes to studying. Discipline is also a needed life skill to have when it comes to college. You will be doing much more work on your own than you did in highschool. This requires good study habits and the perseverance to get through some tough classes.

Survival Tips

Introduction

Congratulations on your acceptance! We are pleased that you chose Umass Boston as your choice of University for next year! Whether you are a freshman or an incoming transfer, you are going to need some survival tips on success. Below you will find some advice that can get you through your first semester.

Get a calendar app or planner to help you stay on track

In your first week of the Semester, you may feel overwhelmed with your workload. It is important to get into the habit of planning a week in advance on when to do homework or study for tests/quizzes.

Pay Attention and Take Notes

It is important that you stay focus on all of your lectures. They will get extremely boring, maybe to the point where you'll accidently miss important notes. If you have trouble concentrating, talk to your professors constantly about what they expect and any material you missed.

Create study guides for exams

Study guides are an important aspect of preparing for exams. Regardless of whether a professor will supply one for you, they can make a difference in your grade. We recommend that you begin working on a study a week before an exam date.

Establish a network of colleagues

To succeed in univeristy life, you have to network. Networking at Umass Boston is much easier than you think. For CSM freshman, you're given the opportunity to join an FSC (Freshman Success Community) which takes the place of your seminar classes and is a gathering of other students with the same major as you.

Resources

Most of your classes will use BlackBoard to store submitted classwork/homework. We recommend that you not only bookmark this website, but also make a routine of accessing the website daily. Keep in mind however that towards the end of a semester, Blackboard will clear all classwork/homework. To save your work from a semester, you need to "artifact" your work which will be shown at the bottom in the submitted work page.

This is a website that you will be visiting often. Not only is Wiser the place where you'll register for classes, but it will have important impormation like final exam dates, final exam dates financial information, and regiudit, and any holds stering for classes, so it is important to contact the right people ASAP to have the hold removed.

Your school emails will be on Microsoft Outlook. We recommend that you download the app for Outlook and keep notifications active. Important imformation and opportunities will be sent to you via Outlook so you MUST check your email daily. Remember to research how to send an email to your professor because you don't want to be too informal and have your email overlooked.

UMBInvolved is a resource where you can access the clubs available at this instituition. We recommend that you check this website out at the start of the semester as clubs will be anticipating new members at that time. UMBInvolved will have information on clubs and contact information if you want to talk more with club-leaders! Being involved in clubs will help amplify your college experience so please take it into consideration!

UMBrella is the research database used by Healey Library. This website contains all the databases you may need in your research papers. We recommend that when you're conducting research, you tinker with the advance search options of each database to see which database works for you. Umbrella will also have information on what physical resources are available at the library and contact informations of the librarians.
